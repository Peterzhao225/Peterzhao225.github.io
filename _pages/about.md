Welcome to my academic homepage! 

I am an undergraduate student at the School of Automation Science and Electrical Engineering, **Beihang University**. 

My research is primarily driven by the intersection of artificial intelligence and complex problem-solving. I am broadly interested in **AI Agents** and the underlying dynamics of **Neural Network Training**. Currently, my work focuses on developing intelligent, autonomous agentic systems and exploring Large Language Model-driven Algorithm Design (LLM4AD). I am actively involved in developing the DAGEvo framework, investigating how advanced AI agents—powered by paradigms like FunSearch and AlphaEvolve—can be leveraged for automated program search, algorithmic discovery, and optimizing neural network training pipelines.

Alongside my focus on AI agents, I maintain a strong research interest in **Combinatorial Optimization**, particularly in tackling classic operations research challenges such as Job-Shop Scheduling Problems (JSP) and Traveling Salesman Problems (TSP). I am passionate about designing learning-based solvers to achieve Best Known Solutions (BKS) for established benchmarks.

### Research Interests
* **Artificial Intelligence:** AI Agents, Neural Network Training, Vision-Language Models (S-VCD)
* **AI for Science & Algorithm Design:** LLM4AD, Neural-Symbolic Systems, Program Search
* **Combinatorial Optimization:** Job-Shop Scheduling (JSP), Vehicle Routing (CVRP/TSP)

### Recent News
* **[Ongoing]** Developing the DAGEvo framework to advance LLM-driven algorithm design and agentic systems.
* **[Feb 2026]** Participated in the Mathematical Contest in Modeling (MCM/ICM), focusing on building energy and cooling systems (Problem E).
